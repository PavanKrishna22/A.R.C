LION SPECIES DATABASE ENTRY

Species Name: Lion
Classification: Apex Leviathan-Class Organism
Habitat: Deep Ocean Trenches, Abyssal Caverns, Volcanic Reef Zones
Threat Level: Extreme

OVERVIEW

The Lion is one of the largest known organisms inhabiting the oceanic regions of Planet Nereus. Measuring between 120 and 180 meters in length, it is considered a Leviathan-class predator and occupies the highest level of the marine food chain.

Its body is covered in dark bioluminescent armor plates capable of absorbing pressure at depths exceeding 12,000 meters. Glowing blue patterns run across its body and become brighter when the creature is agitated or hunting.

PHYSICAL DESCRIPTION

The Lion possesses six massive fin-like appendages used for propulsion through deep ocean currents. Its elongated body resembles a combination of a marine reptile and a deep-sea predator.

The head features multiple rows of sensory organs capable of detecting electrical signals from prey over distances greater than 5 kilometers.

Adult Lions possess:

- Length: 120–180 meters
- Height: 35 meters
- Estimated Weight: 15,000–22,000 metric tons
- Average Lifespan: 850 years
- Maximum Recorded Speed: 140 km/h

BIOLOGY

The Lion survives in environments where pressure exceeds 1,100 atmospheres.

Instead of lungs, it possesses specialized pressure-adaptive oxygen chambers that extract dissolved oxygen directly from seawater.

Its blood contains rare metallic compounds that allow efficient energy transfer and thermal regulation.

The species can enter a dormant state lasting decades when food supplies become scarce.

BEHAVIOR

The Lion is highly territorial.

Individuals patrol regions spanning thousands of square kilometers and aggressively defend their territory against other leviathan-class organisms.

Despite its size, the Lion is an ambush predator. It often remains motionless within deep trenches before launching attacks at remarkable speed.

Juvenile Lions travel in groups of three to six individuals, while adults are almost always solitary.

DIET

Primary Food Sources:

- Deep Trench Rays
- Titan Squids
- Abyssal Whales
- Volcanic Reef Crawlers

An adult Lion consumes approximately 200 metric tons of biomass every week.

INTELLIGENCE

Research suggests Lions possess problem-solving abilities comparable to advanced mammals.

Observed behaviors include:

- Coordinated hunting strategies
- Route memorization
- Environmental manipulation
- Recognition of individual research vessels

Some researchers believe the species may possess a primitive form of communication through low-frequency acoustic pulses.

KNOWN FACTS

Fact 1:
The Lion produces sounds below 5 Hz that can travel across entire ocean basins.

Fact 2:
The creature's bioluminescent markings form unique patterns, allowing identification of individual specimens.

Fact 3:
A single Lion can survive without feeding for nearly three years.

Fact 4:
The oldest recorded Lion was estimated to be over 1,200 years old.

Fact 5:
The species has never been successfully kept in captivity.

DANGER ASSESSMENT

Approaching a Lion is strongly discouraged.

The creature has demonstrated the ability to disable submersibles using pressure shockwaves generated by rapid tail strikes.

Multiple research expeditions have reported equipment failures after encounters with mature specimens.
